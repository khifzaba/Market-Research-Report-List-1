<p><p>Oxygen conservers are categorized into pneumatic and electronic types. Pneumatic oxygen conservers utilize mechanical valves to regulate the flow of oxygen based on the patient's breathing pattern, optimizing usage while ensuring adequate supply. In contrast, electronic oxygen conservers employ sensors and microprocessors to deliver precise dosing and flow adjustments, enhancing efficiency and comfort. Both types are crucial for patients requiring supplemental oxygen, allowing for better mobility and quality of life while minimizing oxygen wastage.</p></p>
