<p><p>A suprapubic catheter is a medical device inserted through the abdominal wall directly into the bladder to facilitate urine drainage. This approach is often employed when urethral catheterization is not feasible or safe, providing an alternative for patients with urethral injuries, obstructions, or chronic urinary retention issues. </p><p>The suprapubic catheter market is witnessing significant growth due to the increasing prevalence of urinary disorders, rising geriatric populations, and advancements in catheter technology. Innovations such as antimicrobial coatings and improved materials are enhancing patient comfort and reducing infection risks. Furthermore, the growing awareness and acceptance of minimally invasive procedures are propelling market demand. </p><p>Healthcare providers are increasingly adopting suprapubic catheters for their benefits, which include reduced urethral trauma and lower infection rates compared to traditional urethral catheters. </p><p>Moreover, the market dynamics are influenced by the development of home healthcare settings, where suprapubic catheters provide convenience for long-term catheterization.
