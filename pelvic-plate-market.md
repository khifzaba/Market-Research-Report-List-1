<p><p>The pelvic plate market is categorized into locking and non-locking segments. Locking pelvic plates utilize specific mechanisms to secure screws tightly, enhancing stability and support for complex fractures. They are preferred in complicated scenarios where optimal alignment is crucial. In contrast, non-locking plates allow for a more flexible fixation, providing adequate stability without the locking feature. This category is often chosen for simpler fractures or situations where bone mobility is desired. Both types cater to diverse surgical needs and patient conditions.</p></p>
