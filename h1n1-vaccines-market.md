<p><p>The H1N1 vaccines market is categorized into three main types: intramuscular, intranasal, and intradermal vaccines. Intramuscular vaccines are delivered via injection into the muscle, providing robust immunity and are commonly used in mass vaccination campaigns. Intranasal vaccines offer a needle-free alternative, administered through the nasal spray, making them easier for patients, especially children. Intradermal vaccines are injected into the dermis, allowing for a lower dose of antigen while still producing a strong immune response, potentially increasing vaccine accessibility.</p></p>
