<p><p>The sphygmomanometer market encompasses three main types: mercury, aneroid, and digital sphygmomanometers. Mercury sphygmomanometers are traditional devices offering high accuracy but facing regulatory challenges due to mercury toxicity. Aneroid sphygmomanometers use mechanical gauges and are portable, commonly used in clinical settings. Digital sphygmomanometers are user-friendly, providing automatic readings and often featuring advanced measurement technology, making them popular for home use. Each type serves specific needs in monitoring blood pressure, contributing to their distinct market segments.</p></p>
